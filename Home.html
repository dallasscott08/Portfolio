About

Ever since my parents purchased our first household computer, all aspects of the digital frontier have intrigued me. As I grew up, my fascination with computers became much deeper. I began to move away from simply using the computer and delved into learning how they worked. After countless hours of tinkering with and fixing my computer, I realized that I could learn to make my own software. This realization led me to the decision to pursue a degree in Computer Science.

I am currently living down in Monroe, Louisiana where I work at CenturyLink as a Software Developer. Before that I worked in downtown Chicago as a Technical Specialist for a British Software company that specializes in OCR technology. In 2012, received my Bachelor’s of Science degree in Computer Science from St. John’s University in Queens, New York. While attending St. John’s, I worked as the Business Manager for The Torch, the school’s award-winning student newspaper, for my sophomore and junior years. I am currently looking for an junior or mid level Software Engineering position and am willing to relocate if necessary. I would like to join an organization that offers strong opportunities for personal growth and advancement.

Beyond programming, I also play the guitar, enjoy working out and personal fitness, and love to kick back with a great video game. I recently released an Android application called Iron Archive which can be downloaded for free from Amazon. I’m a hard working and self-motivated individual who constantly seeks out ways to get my tasks accomplished in the most efficient manner possible.
